B.K.S. Iyengar Banishes My Fear in Sirsasana
by Bruce M. Roger
[photo: BR_BKS_Sirsa1-84.jpg]

August 14, 2015

Re: Guruji2015 anecdote submission



During the first week of the January, 1984 Intensive Course taught by Geetaji, we were expected to balance in Sirsasana in the middle of the room for ten minutes. I had practiced it — leaning against a wall — for only about a month. I was terrified of falling.

Unbeknownst to me, Guruji had started to silently adjust students. When he tried to straighten me out, I struggled to remain upright. Knocking me over he growled, “How dare you resist!” I thought, “Oh, no! My mother told me not to come here, and now Guruji is mad at me.”

Seeking redemption, I looked him in the eye and responded, “Let’s try it again.” He lifted me up, took me through all the twisting variations with a grace and lightness I’d never felt before, and then said softly, “You can come down now.” With his deft touch, my fear vanished and I never sought the security of the wall again.

A week or two later he asked for a demonstrator to make a point. With nothing to lose, I eagerly raised my hand, “I’ll do it.” “The worst one!” he countered. Then he taught how to lift the inner heels, as I struggled to do in the photo.

His delight was in serving others. He was a giant among men. It’s hard to imagine the world without our Guruji, B.K.S. Iyengar.

May his memory be a blessing.
