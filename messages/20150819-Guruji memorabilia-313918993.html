In response for your invitation to supply memorabilia of Guruji, please see photos at the following link: https://drive.google.com/folderview?id=0B29iSQF2GARyNUpWSkFLNXZFaWs&usp=sharing

The photos are of the Lilyfield community centre (in Sydney) where I taught yoga throughout the 1990s, and so too did several others before me, including Pixie Lillas. In 1996 a new mural was to be painted on the outside wall.  It would represents the activities and history of the area, and was to include a yoga pose. I was keen that it was a pose that demonstrated yoga as I knew it, so volunteered to paint Guruji doing Virabadrasana 1. It remains there to this day (a miracle, as the previous two murals on the wall only lasted a few years), and recently I took these photos. They are high resolution, and should reproduce well in the printed form. 

I imagine that you would want to use the photo that is of the mural only (Guruji), but have included a couple of others: the person doing Virabadra facing Guruji is me (the artist), as is the one "adjusting" his back leg (just for fun - you probably won't want to use that one!).  I felt rather cheeky doing the pose opposite him, but after I saw the photo I realized it was actually a useful exercise to look and see where his pose flows and mine doesn't (though I am in jeans and not in the middle of a practice, so not doing my best pose). Painting it 20 years ago was also instructive - getting involved in the skin and the muscle tone in a very observant way.

If you have any trouble viewing/downloading the photos, please let me know. Feel free to use, not use, crop, as you want etc.

After this, in 1997, I went to Canberra (Australia's capital city) to open the first Iyengar Yoga school there. At John Leobold's suggestion, I wrote to Guruji and told him (John said he would like to know). Guruji wrote back, and the letter is characteristic of him, so I am putting a scan of it on my Google Drive along with the pics of the community centre: https://drive.google.com/folderview?id=0B29iSQF2GARyTE1OQkVURGcxbDg&usp=sharing
 Again, feel free to use it, if you will. 

Any trouble down-loading , please do not hesitate to let me know.

In loving memory,

Nameste
