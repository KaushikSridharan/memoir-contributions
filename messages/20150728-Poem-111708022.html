Dear Geetaji and Prasantji,

A poem in memory of your beloved father, Sri BKS Iyengar.


My Teacher’s Teacher                        
 
My teacher’s teacher turned 95. 
Some years ago, for the first time,
He held his mind in his hand.  
As he said this, he held his mind
In his right hand—as a peach, say.
What then? he asked, having known 
Every crevice and detour and stream,
Every mountain and fleck of sand, 
Each creeping inch and headlong mile,
The interior of his every dream
And impulse and wound and word.
 
His chest was broad, and deep
From front to back, the way
That certain men of a great age
Seem to fill the air beyond them
Like a miraculous suit of clothing—
Invisible, and plain for all to see.
It’s no secret what he said then, 
Gentle as a mother’s first breath:
Peek in. As if there is more
And more to the heart's core.


With love and respect, always
Josephine McKendry
