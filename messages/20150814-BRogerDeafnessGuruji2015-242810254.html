B.K.S. Iyengar Cures Deafness with Fingers

Bruce M. Roger

[BKS UDhan 7-86LR.jpg]

 

August 14, 2015

 

Re: Guruji2015 anecdote submission #2

 

While shooting photos of the medical classes in July, 1986, I watched Guruji do something very unusual: sitting behind a young woman, he inserted a finger into each of her ears.

 

Not wanting to call attention to myself, I ignored the pleas of a couple of friends sitting on the stairs. “Hey, Bruce, ask him what he is doing,” they implored repeatedly. Each time they asked, I told them that I didn’t want to interrupt. Finally, to silence them I relented.

 

“Excuse me, Guruji, what are you doing?” I inquired.

 

“Fool, can’t you see? I have my fingers in her ears.” Seeing me chastened, he continued, “Before she was deaf. Now she can hear. That is God’s gift to me.”

 

This was the lovely young woman that he described in Tree of Yoga. I would love to hear Geetaji’s explanation of why he used the backbending in this photo to further open her ears.

 

The guiding light and pioneering work of our Guruji, B.K.S. Iyengar, has inspired generations of yoga students.

 

My wife and I, along with our students at Yoga St. Louis, extend our heartfelt condolences to Geetaji and Prashantji.

 

May his memory be a blessing.
