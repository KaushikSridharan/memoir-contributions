In November 2008, I went to Pune to study at RIMYI and also go on the 90th birthday tour.  I also took some yoga puzzle books that I had created and I wanted to get Guruji’s approval to publish them.  I was very nervous about asking Guruji to approve the books, mainly because I had never had a conversation with him.  I was worried I would have to say “what” because I don’t hear well out of one ear!  I didn’t want to ask Guruji to repeat himself!   I was told by Eddy Marks that the best time to ask him was in the library after he finishes his work.  I went to the library on numerous occasions and sat there trying to read and focus and wait for the right moment and left many times, feeling defeated because I would chicken-out!  After about three weeks of this I finally decide that I’m just going to go to the library and present the books to Guruji!  I enter the library and this day there are only a few people.   Guruji is very busy writing something so I do not interrupt.  I choose Astadala Yogamala 5 and take a seat closest to his desk.  After a bit, Parvaaz comes in with two young ladies and introduces them to Guruji.  They are students from some college and they are asking Guruji about the institute, in regards to the building.  I believe he said it was 3000 square feet total and he was telling them how he acquired the land.  He said the library wasn’t there until later and he decided to make the library underground because it would be quieter.  There was more, but my hearing was limited.  They thanked him, touched his feet and left.  More people came and spoke to Guruji and I continue to read, probably the same page over and over!  I look at the clock and it’s 5:20 and I have class at 6.  I realize I need to make my move!  Guruji takes off his glasses and is putting them in the case.  He takes a breath like he is finished with his work. I look at him and he looks right into my eyes and smiles.  I maintain eye contact with him and move to the chair directly in front of him and say “Guruji, may I give you something?”  He nods yes so I hand him the envelope that says on the front “To Guruji, for your approval”.  I say “I’ve made some puzzle books using your books as the answer keys.  They are a fun way for people to learn Sanskrit”.  He takes the package and says “I will see” and I even say something else but don’t’ remember what because I’m so nervous.  He sits the package to the side.  I thank him and take my book to the librarian and as I’m leaving I bow down to him and thank him again.  I’m ready to hop out of there I’m so happy to be finally leaving the library without the puzzle books!  So the next day I receive a letter from Guruji  He says “It is really a fascinating idea to have brought out this project in the form of puzzles, and I am sure this is bound to create lot of interest not only in the minds of the students of yoga, but also the lovers of yoga....I am sure you have started a good work and I hope it would be appreciated by all which give them time to sit and work on puzzles with patience and tolerance”.    A couple of days later I run into Paul Cabanis and Marla Apt and they tell me that Guruji was showing them the puzzle books in the library.  He says he went on about them for about five minutes and he really liked them.   Marla added that it was real cute because Guruji was thinking of ways they could be used by teachers preparing for assessment, and for children and how people could play puzzles for entertainment instead of watching tv! Later that day I went to the library to thank Guruji for my letter.  At first I didn’t’ think he recognized me but I showed him the letter and he said “oh yes, you are doing good work”.  For me, those words made me so happy!  It made all my nervous hours in the library worthwhile just to hear that!    At his birthday celebration we all lined up to pay homage and when I bowed in front of him he said again, “you are doing good work”.  I keep his words in my heart and mind each day as I practice and teach and cook meals and live my life.   I’m so thankful that he smiled at me that day and gave me the opening I needed to move to him instead of shy away!  Thank you, Guruji!
