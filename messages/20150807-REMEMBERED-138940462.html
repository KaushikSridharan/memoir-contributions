The complexity of describing B.K.S Iyengar comes from the vastness of his interests in so many subjects  as well as the depth of his commitment to the subject of yoga.
In my first asana class with him he devoted great deal of energy to a man with only one leg.
I came to find out he had donated money to dig a well for a leper colony.
I watched as he gave comfort to a woman  in a hospital on her deathbed.
I witnessed his personal trials as he tortured his own body to allow others a pain free life.

His zest for life brought him into sports cars, musical and sporting events,and  onto horseback .
He toured temples, mosques, and churches all over the face of the earth.

His interest in humanity allowed for extensive reading, sightseeing, movies and television. 

He did not live apart from this world but immersed himself into it with a fullness rarely touched by others. 

He took up the subject of yoga to better himself and broadened it to make the world better for all mankind.

How many of us can live so fully?
Manouso
