Dear Geeta and Prashant,

 

I thank you for the invitation to share our individual experiences in reference to our revered master Guruji Iyengar. A world of emotions aroused when we are asked to share about Guruji. 

We all have been touch by him even me, that never had the blessing of being with him. I dear to share my inmost story, as a way of honoring him.

 

I practice the Iyengar Method since 2001, when I arrived to Europe from Argentina, I was 26 years old and I took my first class with a certified teacher in Brussels.

 

I was suddenly having a flash back to when I was six years old when I experienced backbends for the first time. I felt a mix of happiness, freedom and sense of fear dealing with the insecurity and the unknown. 20 years have gone by through many movement and techniques; I was still searching for the guide to walk through those spaces. After the class, I heard the internal voice saying “finally…”  Many like me, were discovering this new world, and we took on to practice intensely, together. 

 

After three years, I became pregnant with twins. The pregnancy and the delivery where natural and with easy… After that with my new life circumstances, I couldn’t found space and time to continue practicing. But when one begins this path, there is the consciousness and the thirst for it, forever!  

 

Within 10 years period I had four dreams about Guruji.

 

In my first dream with B.K.S. Iyengar, he tough me Utthita Trikonasana! I woke up energized, enthusiastic and inspired.  It was a beautiful and graceful restarting point. 

 

The second dream came some years after the first one, I was also at cross roads again, in my life.

I had completed the teacher training in Brussels and because of family needs I was coming back to Argentina and rejecting the opportunity to go to Pune. In Cordoba, my birth town, there weren’t any Iyengar teachers in the whole city, neither in the entire province. 

In that dream, Guruji was teaching me among others in an open field with tables, chairs and high stools. I waked up fortified with the clear challenge of deepening the study without a regular teacher and the mission of sharing all the knowledge, method and philosophy I was taught.

 

The third dream came just some months after turning down the second opportunity to go to Pune in August 2011. It was a new pregnancy, very important for consolidation of the family, but very hard for the practitioner. 

This dream was very special and since them it has accompanying me… It was a tree, a very big and old tree……sitting on the root it was Guruji with you, Geeta. Both silent, with eyes closed, beautiful Samadhi faces… Guruji started melting with... fusing with the tree trunk…

 

I woke up feeling honored by the dream, understanding however, that it would be very improbable for me to know him personally.

The news of his departure became 3 days before my 40th birthday. It was a big impact, a huge sadness…suddenly his presence, his omnipresence was touchable, the thank fullness manifested in endless tears…

 

I understood after the sadness, that I was invited to the vastest inner experience; that I have received maps and tools for exploration to flourish… I could smell the finite fusing the infinite… I was like a furthest blood vessel nourished by him with same love and care than the closest or like an interoceptor of this bigger body that became B.K.S. Iyengar.    

 

When I thought no more dreams will occurred, one come, I dreamed again. He taught me Virabadhrasana III…! The entire progression! I was left with a sense of wonder and gratitude for this connection with this master that fulfill tremendously my life and those of thousands of people around the world. 

 

With respect, love and hope of visiting you in Pune,

 

Luciana Tejedor

Yoga B.K.S. Iyengar Córdoba, Argentina
